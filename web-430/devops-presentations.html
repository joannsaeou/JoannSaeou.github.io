Defining Lead Time vs. Processing Time

Lead time is one of two measures commonly used to measure performance in value streams, with the other being processing time (sometimes known as touch time or task time).

The clock during the Lead Time starts when the request is being made or submitted and ends when it is completely finished.

The processing time is when the clock begins only when youstart working on the customer request. The processing Time just erases the time that the work is in queue that is waiting to be processed.

The Common Scenario: Deployment Lead Timers Requiring Months
The leader timers will take months to possibly 6 month from the time it takes to go from code committed to code successfully running in production.

There is a metric in lead timers by capturing the time at which each changes was initiated in CodePipeline and then updating the row with the time at which that same alteration successfully runs the last action of the deployment pipeline.

Our DevOps Ideal: Deployment Lead Times of Minutes

How lead deployment lead times takes minute? It is by keep checking small code changes. performing testing against it, and deploying it into production.

This will enables your team or work-group to have a high degree of confidence that the changes will operate as designed in production and this is how it can be quickly detected and corrected;

It will take from commit stage then the automated test in 10 minute, to exploratory test in 10 minue and then thee production deployment in 5 minute that is the total cycle of 25minute of deployment lead times,
